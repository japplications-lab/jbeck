Q: How often does jMouse move the mouse?

Every 60 seconds. This interval is fixed and cannot be changed.

Q: Will I notice the mouse moving?

Barely. The movements are only ¬±5 pixels in a random direction ‚Äî it's intentionally subtle to avoid disrupting your work.

Q: Can I change the movement interval (make it faster or slower)?

No, the 60-second interval is hardcoded. This was a design choice to keep the app simple and prevent misuse.

Q: Does jMouse prevent my computer from sleeping?

It prevents automatic sleep/idle timeouts triggered by inactivity. However:

  • ‚úÖ Prevents: Automatic screen lock, screensaver, idle sleep
  • ‚ùå Does not prevent: Manual lock (Win+L), closing laptop lid, power button sleep
Q: What is the F15 key?

F15 is a virtual key that exists in Windows but is not present on most keyboards. Pressing it has no effect on applications, making it perfect for simulating activity without disrupting your work.

Q: Can I schedule jMouse to turn off automatically?

Yes! Enable the "Scheduled End Time" checkbox and set your desired time. jMouse will automatically disable itself when that time arrives.

Q: Can I run jMouse in the background?

Yes. Minimize the window and choose "Yes (System Tray)". jMouse will continue running in the system tray (near your clock). Double-click the tray icon to restore the window.

Q: Does jMouse keep me "active" in Microsoft Teams?

Usually, yes. jMouse simulates system-level activity, which prevents most apps (including Teams) from marking you as "Away". However:

  • Some apps have their own inactivity detection that may override system activity
  • Results may vary depending on your Teams settings and organization policies

jMouse works for most users, but it's not guaranteed to work in 100% of cases.

Q: jMouse isn't working. My screen still locks.

Try the following:

  1. Make sure jMouse is enabled (the status should show "Enabled" in green)
  2. Check if your antivirus quarantined jMouse
  3. Verify that Windows didn't block the app from running
  4. Restart jMouse and re-enable it
Q: jMouse stopped working after a while.

This usually happens if:

  • Your antivirus quarantined the app
  • The scheduled end time was reached
  • You accidentally disabled it via the system tray

Check the system tray icon and verify jMouse is still running and enabled.

Q: The app won't launch. Nothing happens when I double-click.

Check your antivirus. It may have blocked or deleted the file. Restore jMouse from quarantine and add it to your exclusion list.

Q: Can I delete the settings.json file?

Yes. The file is located at:

C:\Users\[YourUsername]\AppData\Roaming\jMouse\settings.json

Deleting it will reset jMouse to default settings (the only thing stored is your minimize-to-tray preference).
